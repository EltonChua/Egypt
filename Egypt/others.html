Ancient Egyptian Games & Activities

Explore traditional games and crafts from the time of the Pharaohs

SENET: Game of the Pharaohs (3000 BCE)

Senet pits two players against each other in a race to the afterlife, each with five souls in their care, subject to the whims of fate represented by four stick dice. This ancient game shares elements with modern games like Backgammon and Pachisi.

  • Perfect for family game nights
  • Quick enough to be a filler game
  • Adaptable to tournament play

Hounds and Jackals

At least 3500 years old, this race game was discovered in multiple variants across Egypt. While the original rules are unknown, historians have reconstructed possible ways to play.

  • Features elimination of opponent's pieces
  • Includes stick dice mechanics
  • Multiple rule variations available

Mehen: The Coiled Snake Game

The earliest known multi-player board game (over 4000 years old) that embodies aspects of early Egyptian religion. Various rule interpretations exist:

  • Simple race game versions
  • Complex positional variants
  • Capture mechanics

Ancient Papyrus Making

Learn how the Egyptians created their revolutionary writing material:

  1. Cut strips from the Papyrus plant
  2. Hammer to remove moisture and soak in water
  3. Reduce sugar content to make strips sticky
  4. Weave strips horizontally & vertically
  5. Press together and dry

The result: durable papyrus paper ready for hieroglyphics!
