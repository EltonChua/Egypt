Egyptian Hieroglyphs

The sacred writing system of ancient Egypt

Hieroglyphs were the formal writing system used in Ancient Egypt that combined logographic and alphabetic elements. Used from around 3200 BCE to 400 CE, hieroglyphs were primarily written on papyrus or carved in stone on monuments and tombs.

Understanding Hieroglyphs

The word "hieroglyph" comes from the Greek words "hieros" (sacred) and "glyphein" (to carve). The ancient Egyptians called their writing "medu netjer" (words of the gods). Hieroglyphs could be written:

  • Left to right
  • Right to left
  • Top to bottom

The Rosetta Stone

The key to deciphering hieroglyphs came with the discovery of the Rosetta Stone in 1799. This granodiorite stele contained the same text in three scripts:

  1. Ancient Egyptian hieroglyphs
  2. Demotic script
  3. Ancient Greek
