Egypt Timeline

The most specific timeline.

c. 3100 BC

Unification of Egypt

Narmer (Menes) unifies Upper and Lower Egypt, establishing the First Dynasty and beginning the Pharaonic era.

c. 2686–2181 BC

Old Kingdom

The era of pyramid building, including the construction of the Great Pyramid of Giza. Pharaohs like Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaure ruled during this period.

c. 2181–2055 BC

First Intermediate Period

A time of political instability and decentralization after the Old Kingdom's decline.

c. 2055–1650 BC

Middle Kingdom

Reunification of Egypt; significant developments in art, literature, and architecture. The capital moves to Thebes.

c. 1550–1070 BC

New Kingdom

Egypt reaches its greatest territorial extent. Famous pharaohs include Hatshepsut, Thutmose III, Akhenaten, and Ramses II. Construction of temples like Karnak and Luxor.

1070–664 BC

Third Intermediate Period

Decline of central authority; division and foreign influence increase.

664–332 BC

Late Period

Brief revival; invasions by Persians, including the time of Persian rule.

332 BC

Conquest by Alexander the Great

Beginning of the Hellenistic period; Alexandria founded.

30 BC

Egypt becomes Roman Province

Cleopatra VII's reign ends; Egypt falls under Roman control.

7th century AD

Arab Conquest

Islam arrives; Egypt becomes part of the Islamic Caliphates.

1517

Ottoman Rule Begins

Egypt becomes part of the Ottoman Empire until 19th century.

19th century

Modernization

Muhammad Ali Pasha establishes a semi-independent state, modernizing Egypt.

1952

Egyptian Revolution

End of monarchy; declaration of the Egyptian Republic.

1956

Suez Crisis

Nationalization of the Suez Canal; military conflict with Britain, France, and Israel.

1979

Egypt-Israel Peace Treaty

Egypt becomes the first Arab country to officially recognize Israel.

21st Century

Recent Developments

Political upheavals during the Arab Spring (2011). Ongoing modernization and economic development.
